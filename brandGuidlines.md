# Elura — Brand Guidelines (Waitlist Phase)

## Brand Name
Elura

---

## Brand Meaning
Elura represents:
- Effortlessness
- Elegance
- Access

It should feel clean, premium, and professional.

---

## Brand Overview
Elura is a platform that helps makeup artists get discovered and booked easily.

At the waitlist stage, the goal is to attract high-quality makeup artists and validate demand.

---

## Core Message
"Get more clients without relying on Instagram."

---

## Tagline
"Get discovered. Get booked."

---

## Positioning
Elura is:
- A professional booking and discovery platform for beauty artists

Elura is NOT:
- A social media platform
- A generic portfolio site

---

## Target Audience (Primary)
- Makeup artists (beginner to professional)
- Location-focused (start with a single city e.g. Lagos)

---

## Tone of Voice
- Clear and direct
- Confident
- Minimal
- Slightly premium

### Examples

Bad:
"Join our amazing platform for beauty creatives!!!"

Good:
"Get booked as a makeup artist without chasing clients."

---

## Visual Style (Waitlist Only)

### Colors
- Background: #0B0B0F (Deep black)
- Text: #FFFFFF (White)
- Accent: #E8AEB7 (Soft pink)

---

### Typography
- Headings: Bold, clean (e.g. Inter, Satoshi)
- Body: Simple and readable (e.g. Inter)

---

## Layout Principles
- Minimal sections
- Strong whitespace
- Focus on real makeup portfolio images
- No clutter or unnecessary UI elements

---

## Key Sections for Waitlist Page

1. Hero
   - Headline: "Get booked as a makeup artist — without chasing clients."
   - Subtext: "Join early access for top artists in Lagos."
   - CTA: "Join as an Artist"

2. Problem
   - Instagram is crowded
   - Hard to get consistent clients
   - Booking is unstructured

3. Solution
   - Professional portfolio
   - Direct booking system
   - Verified trust layer

4. Form
   - Clean and simple
   - Focused on artist onboarding

---

## Success Metrics
- Number of artist signups
- Quality of signups
- Strong concentration in one launch location

---

## What to Avoid
- Overdesigning
- Generic startup language
- Trying to serve artists and clients equally at this stage
- Building features into the waitlist

---

## Brand Rule
Everything should reinforce this idea:

"Elura makes artists feel like professionals and helps them get booked."
